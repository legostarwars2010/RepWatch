Data Sources

RepWatch aggregates official voting records and legislative data from trusted government sources to ensure accuracy and reliability.

Primary Sources

U.S. House Clerk

Official vote records from the Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives. We download XML roll call files directly from clerk.house.gov.

Senate.gov

Senate voting records retrieved from the official Senate Legislative Information System at senate.gov.

Congress.gov

Bill text, summaries, and legislative metadata sourced from the Library of Congress via Congress.gov.

U.S. Census Bureau TIGERweb

Congressional district boundaries and geographic data used for address-to-district resolution, provided by the U.S. Census Bureau.

AI-Powered Summaries

Bill summaries are generated using OpenAI's GPT models to translate complex legislative language into plain English. All AI-generated content is clearly labeled and based on official bill text from Congress.gov.

Data Freshness

Vote data is updated periodically from official sources. RepWatch does not modify or editorialize voting records—we display them exactly as reported by the government.

Open & Transparent

RepWatch is committed to transparency. Our data pipeline is documented, and we only use publicly available government sources. No proprietary data. No hidden algorithms.
